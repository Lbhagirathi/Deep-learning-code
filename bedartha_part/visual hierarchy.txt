gessalt principle: the more similar two elements are, the more likely they are to be perceived as part of the same group 

The Gestalt principles describe how our brain naturally organizes visual information into meaningful patterns rather than seeing separate elements.

When someone says “different colors (inline with Gestalt principle)”, they’re usually referring to one specific rule within Gestalt:

🎨 Law of Similarity

Objects that share similar features—like color, shape, or size—are perceived as part of the same group.

Same color → seen as related
Different colors → seen as separate groups

👉 Example:
If you have a chart where some points are blue and others are red, your brain instantly groups all blue points together and all red points together—even if they’re mixed spatially.

Other key Gestalt principles (quick context)
Proximity → things close together feel grouped
Continuity → we prefer smooth, continuous lines
Closure → we “fill in” missing parts of shapes
Figure–ground → we separate object from background
Why it matters

Using different colors intentionally helps:

Improve clarity in graphs or UI
Highlight categories or patterns
Guide attention without extra explanation
